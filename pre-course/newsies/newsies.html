TRUMP PREVAILS IN SHOCKER

By JULIE PACE and

ROBERT FURLOW

Associated Press

WASHINGTON - In a stunning and historic upset, Donald J. Trump, the businessman and reality television star, has been elected the 45th President of the United States. Trump’s victory over former Secretary of State Hillary Clinton marks one of the most shocking political turnarounds in modern American history.

Trump, who ran on a platform of radical change and a promise to "Make America Great Again," secured key victories in battleground states, including Florida, Ohio, and Pennsylvania, ultimately amassing more than the required 270 Electoral College votes.

At his election night headquarters in New York City, Trump addressed an exuberant crowd of supporters just after 3 a.m. “This is not a campaign, this is a movement,” Trump declared. “I will be President for all Americans, and this is so important to me.”

Trump's campaign, characterized by its unorthodox strategies and controversial rhetoric, tapped into a deep well of voter frustration with the political establishment. His promises to renegotiate trade deals, build a wall along the U.S.-Mexico border, and repeal the Affordable Care Act resonated with millions of Americans who felt left behind by the economic recovery and disillusioned by Washington politics.
